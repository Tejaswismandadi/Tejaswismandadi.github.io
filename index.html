About Me

I have always been fascinated by one question:

Why do some companies recover from disruptions within days while others struggle for months—or even years?

That question has shaped my career.

I began as a mechanical design engineer in the automotive industry, where I spent more than a decade developing products, working with suppliers, supporting manufacturing, and solving complex engineering challenges. Over the years, I realized that the biggest obstacles were rarely technical. More often, projects were delayed by supplier failures, capacity shortages, logistics disruptions, financial instability, poor visibility, or risks that no one had identified early enough.

Again and again, I saw talented teams working tirelessly to solve problems after they occurred. I started asking a different question:

What if we could identify these risks before they became crises?

That curiosity led me to pursue a Master of Supply Chain Management at the University of Michigan Ross School of Business. It also became the foundation of my work in supply chain risk and resilience.

Since then, I have focused on studying how organizations identify, assess, prioritize, and respond to supply chain risks. I have explored real disruptions across industries, worked on consulting projects involving supplier risk assessment and resilience frameworks, and developed practical methodologies that organizations can adapt to strengthen their supply chains.

My approach combines three perspectives:

  • Engineering, to understand how products are designed and manufactured.
  • Supply Chain, to understand how suppliers, logistics, inventory, and operations interact.
  • Risk & Resilience, to understand how disruptions propagate through complex global networks and how organizations can prepare for them.

Together, these perspectives allow me to bridge technical detail with strategic decision-making.

I believe risk management should not be a collection of spreadsheets, dashboards, or alerts. It should provide leaders with clear, actionable insight that enables better decisions before disruptions affect customers, operations, or financial performance.

This belief is what inspired me to begin developing practical frameworks and digital solutions that simplify supply chain risk management. My long-term vision is to help organizations move beyond reactive firefighting and build resilient supply chains through better visibility, structured decision-making, and proactive risk management.

This portfolio is a reflection of that journey. It is not simply a collection of projects.

It is a knowledge base built from research, real-world case studies, consulting experience, and continuous learning. Every framework, methodology, and tool presented here has one purpose: helping organizations understand risk, make informed decisions, and build stronger, more resilient supply chains.

I believe resilience is not built when disruption occurs. It is built through the decisions we make every day before disruption ever arrives.
