Why It Exists

Most disruptions are the cost of invisibility, not bad luck.

AGNA opens with a case: NovaDrive Motors, a fictional OEM, loses $180M when a single-source Taiwan semiconductor supplier goes offline. No alternate had been qualified. No recovery time had been calculated. No survival window had been measured. The loss wasn't a surprise — it was a question nobody had ever asked.

That's the gap AGNA is built to close: not another red/yellow/green dashboard, but a system that answers a harder question — which risks are actually measurable, and what to do differently about the ones that aren't.

Grounded in Frank Knight's 1921 distinction between measurable risk and true uncertainty — because applying a probability score to a genuine black swan creates false confidence, which is worse than no system at all.
Methodology

Eleven steps, starting with where the organization actually is.

Rather than assuming every company is ready for full TTR/TTS analysis on day one, AGNA opens with a seven-dimension maturity assessment (Step 0) — so the first deliverable to leadership is an honest picture of where the organization stands, not a generic template.

From there: governance and escalation thresholds set before any risk is scored (Step 1), TTR/TTS criticality testing that narrows a full supplier base to the handful of nodes that matter (Step 5), a structured sub-tier disclosure process built specifically to surface diamond nodes — hidden convergence points where one sub-tier supplier feeds several independent Tier 1 relationships (Step 6) — and a mitigation and monitoring layer that closes with board-ready ROI reporting (Steps 8–11).

On the Platform

Six chapters, an interactive network map, and a live calculator.

An interactive supply network map filterable by tier (All / P1 / P2 / P3 / Diamond Nodes) — click any supplier to see its REI score, TTS-vs-TTR comparison, and a phased mitigation plan. A live risk calculator that takes a supplier profile and inventory inputs and returns a tier, safety-stock recommendation, and mitigation plan in real time. And a full methodology walkthrough covering 17 risk types across 5 categories.

Validated in the Field

The REI framework, tested outside the platform.

The Risk Exposure Index (REI) scoring framework designed within AGNA was subsequently applied during the Nexteer Automotive Ross Consulting Studio engagement — the P1/P2/P3 tiering, TTR/TTS gap analysis, and diamond node concept all trace back to work first done here.

Scope note: AGNA is an independent portfolio project, built and maintained outside of any employer, using a fictional OEM (NovaDrive Motors) as its case study. It is not a client deliverable or work history — it's the methodology laboratory where that work started.
