01 — Situation

Five pressures compounding at once.

ISM-Healthcare's net profit fell from $0.63B to $0.16B — a 74.6% year-over-year collapse — against a 21.5% surge in cost of sales and stagnant 3.8% revenue growth, with performance trailing industry benchmarks across gross margin, operating margin, net margin, and inventory turnover (9.4x against a 10.9x benchmark).

Behind the numbers: tariff escalation on the 62% of medical devices sourced overseas, geopolitical disruption to anesthetics and other critical supply, Medicaid funding cuts disproportionately hitting urban and rural facilities, inventory data siloed across 200+ facilities, and rising workforce turnover straining an already stretched supply chain team.

02 — My Workstream: Sourcing Strategy

Not every supplier needs the same protection.

Designed a tariff-aware, clinical-criticality sourcing segmentation across the network's 200+ facility supply base — the core insight being that a uniform sourcing policy was the wrong tool for a supply base where the cost of getting it wrong varied enormously by category.

Built a two-tier response: mandatory dual-sourcing for Tier A life-critical items — anesthetics, implants, pacemakers — even where dual-sourcing costs more, because the alternative is a canceled surgery; and near-shoring for Tier B tariff-exposed but non-critical consumables, where cost and flexibility could be optimized without touching patient safety.

03 — Where This Fit the Team's Plan

One of five priorities under "Protect Care, Rebound Margin, Build Supply Chain Resilience."

Team SHEQForce's full strategic response spanned five priorities: re-shaping the cost and supplier base, redesigning inventory and network footprint, a rapid digital visibility backbone, a stronger operating model and workforce plan, and government/KPI alignment — organized around facility archetypes (Urban Safety-Net vs. Specialty/Elective Hubs) and a small central "Expert Spine" governance team.

The sourcing and supplier-base workstream — my contribution — was scored in the team's own impact assessment as high-feasibility for 2026 execution, with a projected 150–300 basis point margin recovery through reduced tariff exposure.

1st place, globally.

Team SHEQForce's strategic plan won 1st place against competing teams at ISM World 2026.
